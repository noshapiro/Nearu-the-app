Designer's Note · 09
Why an Orb, Not a Human Face?
The temptation was to render a hyperrealistic human face for Aurora. We deliberately chose an abstract orb for three reasons. First, the Uncanny Valley problem — a near-human face that's slightly "off" erodes trust instantly, the exact opposite of Nearu's core mission. Second, an orb is permission to be better than human — the aurora-gradient skin, orbiting rings, and glowing eyes communicate a presence that is genuinely alien and wonderful, not merely a digital copy. Third, users in research sessions reported feeling less judged by the orb during vulnerable conversations. The abstract form invites openness. The orbiting rings echo the Nearu logo and brand language, making Aurora instantly recognizable as a Nearu creation, not a generic chatbot. The speaking waveform at the mouth position gives Aurora a "voice you can see," which tested significantly higher in emotional engagement than animated lips.
Designer's Note · 10
The PiP Camera is About Accountability
The user's own camera preview isn't just a technical requirement — it's a behavioral design choice. Seeing yourself during a coaching or journaling session with Aurora creates a mild accountability loop. "I can see myself — I should be present." The PiP is sized at exactly 96×130pt: large enough to read your own expression, small enough to not distract from Aurora. The drag-to-reposition interaction with corner snap points lets users move it out of sight on days they don't want that accountability. That optionality is itself a trust signal.
