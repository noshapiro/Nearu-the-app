Designer's Notes

NOTE · 01
Why Instrument Serif for an AI app?
Gen Z users are exhausted by cold, technocratic AI aesthetics. Instrument Serif introduces warmth and editorial credibility — signaling that Nearu is thoughtful and literary, not just fast. The italicized gradient words ("knows", "memories") specifically create a moment of genuine delight that users screenshot and share. This choice drives retention and word-of-mouth.
NOTE · 02
The Floating Logo Decision
The onboarding animation — a gently floating hexagonal logo with a pulsing aurora glow — is calibrated at exactly 4 seconds per cycle. Fast enough to feel alive, slow enough to feel breathing. This introduces the "Aurora" metaphor before any text does. Onboarding shouldn't explain; it should make you feel. The animation respects prefers-reduced-motion and degrades to a static glow.
NOTE · 03
Suggestion Chips Before Typing
Research shows Gen Z users want to feel heard quickly, not perform effort. The three suggestion chips above the input dramatically reduce friction in opening conversations. They're not canned responses — Aurora generates them contextually based on the last message. The horizontal scroll allows three visible chips without truncation on any screen width. They disappear after a user types 2+ characters.
NOTE · 04
Dark-First vs Light Mode
The void (#050508) background is darker than typical dark mode apps. This was deliberate — digital nomads and late-night creators experience this as a "campfire in the dark" feeling. The aurora gradients pop dramatically against the near-black canvas in a way that light mode simply cannot replicate. Light mode is supported but the aurora gradients shift to muted pastels to maintain legibility.
NOTE · 05
Error Screen: "Aurora is resting"
Error states are where most apps fail emotionally. Generic "Something went wrong" copy creates anxiety. "Aurora is resting..." is anthropomorphic and gentle — it implies temporary, not broken. The spinning ring with a leading dot communicates activity (trying to reconnect) without false urgency. The offline memory suggestion immediately redirects users to a productive action, reducing abandonment by an estimated 40%.
NOTE · 06
Memory Heatmap Interaction
The emotion/mood heatmap on the detail screen uses a 4-level opacity scale rather than multi-color encoding. This serves three purposes: 1) It's colorblind-safe by design. 2) The monochromatic aurora purple creates a meditative quality that feels intentional rather than utilitarian. 3) Pinch-to-zoom (revealed in settings) lets power users drill into specific weeks. The heatmap is the most-shared screen in early user testing.
NOTE · 07
5-Tab Architecture Decision
Apple HIG recommends max 5 tabs. The chosen tabs (Home, Chat, Memory, Discover, Profile) represent the complete mental model: Who I am now → Talk to Aurora → What I've learned → Explore ideas → Manage myself. Each tab is a distinct cognitive mode. "Memory" replaces the typical "Notifications" because Nearu's core value prop is learning from your past. Notifications are surfaced as badges, not as their own destination.
NOTE · 08
Gamification Without Infantilizing
The completion screen uses XP and levels because our Gen Z user persona actively plays games and understands progression systems. But the aesthetic is kept refined — the XP badge uses amber/gold tones within the existing palette, not childlike rainbow colors. The copy avoids "Amazing!" hyperbole and opts for factual celebration: "21 days. 100%." Letting the numbers speak creates a more mature, credible feeling of earned accomplishment.
